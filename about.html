ABOUT

Entrepreneurs Against Poverty (EntrAPov) is a network of caring entrepreneurs and virtual interns. Regular people like you and me who assist budding entrepreneurs in impoverished nations. Volunteers provide business expertise combined with Entrapov’s financial support micro-loans into areas where US dollars go a long way. The EntrAPov “vision” became a reality in 2020 when 2 founders and 4 virtual interns helped launch a women’s sandal brand, Chipantha.

FOUNDERS

Ben Davidson, Angel Entrepreneur

Ben Davidson, Co-founder and “Angel Entrepreneur,” runs the non-profit, Entrepreneurs Against Poverty (EntrAPov.com) as a side hobby. He funds entrepreneurs in impoverished nations. Working along side these business owners, Ben lends expertise in web development, copy writing, human resources, and videography. He recently published an Amazon bestseller entitled, Get Hired Guarantee. Speaking regularly on college campuses, Ben shares his entertaining career journey that led him to love helping entrepreneurs in Africa. Ben lives in Idaho where he graduated with an MBA from Idaho State University in 2001. He enjoys the outdoors, art, and philanthropy.

Prince Mensah, Director for Operations & Training West Africa

Prince Mensah leads Entrapov’s West African operations. As Executive Director, he runs the startup incubator, allocates seed money to entrepreneurs, and approves capital expenditures. His startup incubator trains under-employed women and men in Ghana to start and run a business. Mensah identifies the best plans and prepares the founders to pitch their businesses for startup funding. He disperses seed money and tracks financial performance. Mensah manages a large budget, volunteers, and budding entrepreneurs. He is responsible for the growth of Entrapov far beyond my expectations as founder of the non-profit. I trust him completely. He does an outstanding job managing the people, assets, and sharing the vision of Entrepreneurs Against Poverty.

Dalisey Brooks, Co-founder

Dalisey Brooks, Co-Founder, hosts pitch events and tells the Entrapov story over social media and investor communication campaigns. She creates public relations and marketing content to attract American investors and followers of the “Shark Pond” pitch events. She teaches entrepreneurs to write/present compelling business pitches with sound financial projections. She helps match sponsoring investors to startups that fit their interests.
